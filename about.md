I studied sociology and psychology with the goal of becoming a researcher. After I had specialized in a Master's program on economic and quantitative sociology, I withdrew from university to pursue alternative life goals with varying success. Three years later, which is now, I am moving back into academia to pursue a PhD in economic sociology, since all alternatives (business consulting, marketing, managing a night club, landscaping) turned out to be relatively boring. So here I go again.

### More Information

My alma mater is the University of Mannheim (Germany), which equipped me with a strong analytical approach to the social sciences and a huge toolkit filled with methods from quantitative research and computational social science, including various machine learning techniques. Substatively, I have always been interested in the intersection of the social and the economic, since I read Smith's Wealth of Nations and Marx' Capital when I was like sixteen and kind of fell in love with the topics and ways of thinking. Since then, I specialized in the analysis of labor markets, individual careers and wage growth, and societal wage inequality.
